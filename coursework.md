### MAT3280 Probability Theory 概率论
I took this course in 2024 Fall term, taught by Prof. Wingki Shum (Kenneth). Along with MAT2050, it was another excellent course I took at CUHK-Shenzhen. Prof. Shum also wrote his own textbook, [Measure-theoretic Probability: With Applications to Statistics, Finance, and Engineering](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-031-49830-5).<br>

In the first half of the term, the course provided a rigorous introduction to measure-theoretic probability, closely connected with MAT3006 Real Analysis. It began with basic topology, measure concept, and continued through the Lebesgue integral and convergence theorems. 

In the second half, the course covered a broad but still substantial range of topics in probability, including but not limit to: coupling and optimal transport, MGF & CF, modes of convergence, the LLN, and conditional expectation. There were also lectures briefly introducing more advanced concept like martingales and Hilbert spaces.

This course helped me understand probability from a very fundamental measure-theoretic perspective, and also gave me a clearer sense of how modern mathematics is built. It was a rigorous mathematics course that required careful and precise proof writing. In addition, Prof. Shum was very kind and humorous. I received a good grade in the course, and he also helped with my recommendation letters :). Thanks Kenneth!
